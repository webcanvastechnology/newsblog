Bitcoin Worth Over EUR 50 Million Seized From Fraudster Who Refuses to Give Up Password
Ashmawi Sami LightBox Mar 10, 2015 Comments

German prosecutors have confiscated more than EUR 50 million (roughly Rs. 440 crores) worth of Bitcoin from a fraudster. There's only one problem: they can't unlock the money because he won't give them the password. The man was sentenced to jail and has since served his term, maintaining his silence throughout while police made repeated failed efforts to crack the code to access more than 1,700 Bitcoin, said a prosecutor in the Bavarian town of Kempten. "We asked him but he didn't say," prosecutor Sebastian Murer told Reuters on Friday. "Perhaps he doesn't know."

Elon Musk Offers 1,255 Free Ventilators From China

Ashmawi Sami LightBox Mar 10, 2015 Comments

Multi-billionaire tech mogul Elon Musk on Tuesday offered 1,255 ventilators for free to help fight COVID-19 pandemic. "China had an oversupply, so we bought 1255 FDA-approved ResMed, Philips and Medtronic ventilators… and airshipped them to LA. If you want a free ventilator installed, please let us know!" Musk tweeted, offering praise to Tesla's China team. The need for additional ventilators has taken center stage in the ongoing fight against the spread of coronavirus and the ventilator has become a critical first line of defense for patients infected by the virus. Additionally, Musk has donated 50,000 N95 surgical masks and various protective items to a hospital in the US.

Google Fit app will soon track heart rate, respiratory rate using camera on Pixel phones

Ashmawi Sami LightBox Mar 10, 2015 Comments

Google’s Pixel phones will soon be able to track heart rate and respiratory rate using just the camera. The feature will be made available in Google Fit app for Pixel phones, the company announced in a blog post. The feature will be expanded to more Android phones in the future, but it is not specified which devices will get this next. In a blog post, Google’s Shwetak Patel, Director of Health Technologies at Google Health, wrote, the feature will be made available starting next month, and the company will be relying on sensors which are already built into the smartphone such as the “microphone, camera and accelerometer” to track the new health parameters.

Harvard University Launches Free Online Course In Web Programming With Python And JavaScript

Ashmawi Sami LightBox Mar 10, 2015 Comments

Harvard University launches free online course in Web Programming with Python and JavaScript Harvard University has been on a spree to offer free online courses throughout the last year. In its endeavour to provide excellence to the learners, Harvard University has recently rolled out a new free online course in Web Programming with Python and JavaScript. The intermediate-level course will be instructed by David J. Malan, Gordon McKay Professor of the Practice of Computer Science at Harvard University and Brian Yu, Senior Preceptor in Computer Science at Harvard University. The 12-week course is named "CS50's Web Programming with Python and JavaScript" and covers the following aspects of web development - HTML, CSS Git Python Django SQL, Models, and Migrations JavaScript User Interfaces Testing, CI/CD Scalability and Security What is in it for learners This course picks up where CS50 leaves off, diving more deeply into the design and implementation of web apps with Python, JavaScript, and SQL using frameworks like Django, React, and Bootstrap.
